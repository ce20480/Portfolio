January 2028: First Year Trap
Your first Self Assessment includes the full 2026/27 tax bill (~GBP 11,000) PLUS a 50% "payment on account" towards 2027/28 (~GBP 5,500). That's ~150% of one year's tax in a single payment.

This is why the GBP 1,600/month tax reserve matters: by January 2028 (22 months), you'll have ~GBP 35,200 saved — enough for both the January payment and the July 2028 second payment on account.

What is a "payment on account"? When your tax bill exceeds GBP 1,000, HMRC requires advance payments towards next year's tax. Each payment is 50% of your previous year's bill. Due 31 January and 31 July. In your first year, you have no prior payments, so the full bill + first advance hit at once. [GOV.UK: Payments on account]
MTD quarterly dates above are REPORTING deadlines, not payment deadlines. You submit income and expense data to HMRC via FreeAgent. No money changes hands. Tax payments follow the existing Self Assessment schedule: 31 January and 31 July. [GOV.UK: MTD for Income Tax]

MTD quarterly submissions are REPORTING only. They do NOT change when you pay tax. You send income and expense data to HMRC quarterly via FreeAgent. Tax payments remain on the Self Assessment schedule (31 January / 31 July). These are two completely separate things.

Soft landing: No penalty points for late quarterly submissions in the first year of MTD. [GOV.UK: MTD for Income Tax]
